All answers are worth one point unless otherwise indicated.

Each question has the following parts:
  1. Infix notation - "normal" expressions with precedence, parentheses, and left-to-right evaluation.
  2. Postfix notation.
  3. Maximum stack depth. This is how much of the stack would be used if the Postfix expression was evaluated in the normal way, pushing operands on the stack, popping them off to do the operations, and pushing the results back on the stack.
  4. Value. Show the value assuming integer arithmetic.
  5. Tree. Draw the tree reprsentation on the right.

Do not reorder expressions

Do not reorder expressions according to the usual commutative rules. Ie, altho a+b is the same as b+a in normal arithmetic do not reverse the operands. Because of operator overloading, plus may not be what you think it is for objects. Even tho the arithmetic plus can not be overloaded, write the operands in the "correct" order.

Hint. The left-to-right order of the operands will be the same in the infix, postfix, and tree notations.

Fill in the missing parts. To the right show the equivalent expression tree.
